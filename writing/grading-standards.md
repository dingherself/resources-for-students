<h1 class="mb-5">Grading Standards for Objection-Reply Style Essays</h1>

|                                        | 5                                                            | 6                                                            | 7+                                                           |
| :----: | ------------------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| *Motive* | The essay’s title and opening **seem to bear on** a genuine question, puzzle, or problem. | The essay’s title and opening together **establish** a genuine question, puzzle, or problem.<br /><br />This issue is **clearly presented**. | The essay’s title and opening together **establish and address** a genuine question, puzzle, or problem.<br /><br />This issue is not only clearly presented but **thoughtfully motivated in a compelling, well-focused way which genuinely captures the reader’s interest**. |
| *Thesis*                               | The essay **can be read as having** a thesis. For an objection-reply style essay, the thesis is usually an objection, or a reply to an objection, to an existing answer, solution, or response (as appropriate) to the essay’s motivating issue. | The essay then **clearly states** its thesis. For an objection-reply style essay, the thesis is usually an objection, or a reply to an objection, to an existing answer, solution, or response (as appropriate) to the essay’s motivating issue. | The essay then clearly states its thesis. For an objection-reply style essay, the thesis is usually an objection, or a reply to an objection, to an existing answer, solution, or response (as appropriate) to the essay’s motivating issue.<br /><br />The essay’s thesis is both **arguable** (i.e., not obviously true/false) and **insightful** (e.g., building on but extending beyond class discussions). |
| *Key Terms & Concepts*                 | The essay **establishes some** key terms and concepts.       | The essay **clearly establishes most relevant** key terms and concepts. | The essay **clearly establishes, carefully explains, and consistently employs suitable** key terms and concepts, **with reference to the text where appropriate**.<br /><br />The essay introduces jargon **only when and only to the extent helpful to the reader**.<br /><br />When a key term or concept is abstract, complex, or otherwise not obvious, the essay **carefully and thoughtfully illustrates** it with an example, analogy, diagram, or some other heuristic device. |
| *Textual Evidence* | The essay **draws evidence from the text** to support its reading of the view, argument, or idea discussed.<br /><br />These sources are **cited properly**. | The essay **consistently draws evidence** from the text to support its reading of the view, argument, or idea discussed.<br /><br />These sources are cited properly **using a consistent style** (e.g., Chicago Author-Date). | The essay consistently draws **relevant** evidence from the text to support its reading of the view, argument, or idea discussed.<br /><br />These sources are properly cited using a consistent style (e.g., Chicago Author-Date) and **smoothly incorporated into the prose mainly in the form of paraphrases. The few direct quotes, if used, are well-chosen, focused, idiomatic, and explicitly interpreted rather than left to the reader to interpret on their own.** |
| *Interpretation & Exposition* | The essay does not merely report that an interlocutor holds a certain view but **makes an attempt to explain the reason** on the basis of which the interlocutor contends such a view is true.<br /><br />The essay’s exposition **could plausibly be supported** by textual evidence. | The essay does not merely report that an interlocutor holds a certain view but **clearly explains** the reason on the basis of which the interlocutor contends such a view is true.<br /><br />The essay’s exposition **is supported by the textual evidence cited**. | The essay does not merely report that an interlocutor holds a certain view but **clearly and thoughtfully explains** the reason on the basis of which the interlocutor contends such a view is true.<br /><br />The essay’s exposition is **well-supported by a careful, critical, charitable reading** of the textual evidence cited. **Where appropriate, the essay clearly tells the reader how its exposition is so supported**. |
| *Objection–Reply* | The essay **can be interpreted as offering** some objection to an argument or some reply to an objection. | The essay **clearly presents** an objection to an argument or a reply to an objection.<br /><br />The essay’s own claims are not merely asserted but **often argued for**.<br /><br />The essay **displays some awareness of and engages with some** countervailing considerations, alternative positions, or further plausible worries or objections. | The essay clearly presents a **compelling, insightful** objection to an argument or a reply to an objection.<br /><br />The essay’s own claims are not merely asserted but **well–argued for**. **Where appropriate, the essay clearly tells the reader how its claims are supported by the arguments**.<br /><br />The essay displays a **sophisticated** awareness of and, **where appropriate**, engages with countervailing considerations, alternative positions, or further plausible worries or objections. |
| *Structure & Paragraphing* | The essay and its sections are **titled**.<br /><br />Different components of the essay are **identifiable**. <br /><br />Individual paragraphs are **largely internally coherent**. | The essay and its sections are **appropriately titled to indicate the main content and structure of the essay**.<br /><br />Different components of the essay are **clearly identifiable**. <br /><br />Each paragraph is **internally coherent and makes a single point**. | The essay and its sections are **thoughtfully titled** to indicate the main content and structure of the essay in a way **helpful and interesting to the reader**.<br /><br />Different components of the essay are clearly **identified**. <br /><br />Each paragraph is internally coherent, makes a single point, **serves its own unique, useful purpose, and builds on each other**. |
| *Organization & Signposting* | The essay **can be read as following a more or less coherent** internal logic. | The essay **follows a coherent, sensible** internal logic.<br /><br />The essay **employs** signposting language **at major points of transition**. | The essay **consciously develops its own fitting, progressive overarching line of thought in response to its motivating issue**.<br /><br />The essay **thoughtfully employs** signposting language **where appropriate and helpful to the reader**. |
| *Style & Conventions* | The essay is composed in **largely clear** prose.<br /><br />The essay demonstrates a **largely proficient** command of conventional syntax, usage, spelling, and punctuation, as well as scholarly and philosophical conventions on diction, voice, tone, format, and citation style. | The essay is **largely free of obscurities and develops a more or less distinct voice**.<br /><br />The essay demonstrates a **proficient** command of conventional syntax, usage, spelling, and punctuation, as well as scholarly and philosophical conventions on diction, voice, tone, format, and citation style. | The essay is composed in **lucid, well-chosen, engaging, and polished prose with negligible obscurities and sustains a unique, interesting voice**.<br /><br />The essay demonstrates a **smooth and skillful** command of—and **where appropriate and useful, may sometimes deliberately break away from**—conventional syntax, usage, spelling, and punctuation, as well as scholarly and philosophical conventions on diction, voice, tone, format, and citation style. |
| *Research (if relevant)* | If it is a research paper, the essay **mentions some external sources**. | If it is a research paper, the essay **engages some relevant literature and makes an attempt to situate itself in relation to it**. | If it is a research paper, the essay **selectively engages and thoughtfully critiques existing scholarship in an effort to fill some gap, resolve some problem, or otherwise make some contribution of its own**. |
